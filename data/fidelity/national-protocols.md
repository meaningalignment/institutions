# Shared standards for mandate articulation

To apply the value of fidelity broadly, a society needs shared infrastructure: standards for articulating what institutions are for (in terms thick enough to be contestable), standards for measuring whether they are serving those purposes, and channels for practitioner and stakeholder signal.

Design questions:
- Who builds and maintains the articulations? Industry bodies, independent standards organizations, something analogous to the Financial Accounting Standards Board?
- How do mandates stay revisable? Who can contest a national specification, and through what procedure?
- How is measurement kept from becoming another thin-metric regime? Without careful articulation, "fidelity measurement" degrades into new Goodhart targets.
- How is this infrastructure kept independent of political cycles?

Analogues worth studying: GAAP and accounting standards; national health-outcome registries; judicial interpretive traditions around constitutional text; the metrology infrastructure maintained by national standards agencies. The goal is not a national ministry of fidelity but shared public infrastructure that supports contestation at every scale below it.
