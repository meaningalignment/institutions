# Accreditation & Epistemic Authority

## Textbooks

- **Collins, H. & Evans, R.** — *Rethinking Expertise* (2007). Taxonomy of expertise types and their social roles.
- **Jasanoff, S.** — *The Fifth Branch: Science Advisers as Policymakers* (1990). How scientific expertise gets institutionalized in governance.
- **Tetlock, P.** — *Expert Political Judgment* (2005). Empirical study of when expert forecasts succeed and fail.
- **Turner, S. P.** — *Liberal Democracy 3.0: Civil Society in an Age of Experts* (2003). Tension between expertise and democratic legitimacy.

## Tutorials & Courses

- [ABET Accreditation](https://www.abet.org/) — How engineering programs get accredited.
- [ISO/IEC 17011](https://www.iso.org/standard/67198.html) — International standard for accreditation bodies.

## Key Concepts

- **Credentialing** — certifying individuals as competent in a domain.
- **Accreditation** — certifying that institutions meet quality standards.
- **Epistemic authority** — legitimate claim to be believed on a subject.
- **Calibration** — whether confidence levels match actual accuracy.
- **Peer review** — expert evaluation of expert work.
