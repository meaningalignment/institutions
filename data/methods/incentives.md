# Mechanism Design & Market Design

## Textbooks

- **Börgers, T.** — *An Introduction to the Theory of Mechanism Design* (2015). Clear graduate-level textbook on mechanism design theory.
- **Roth, A. E.** — *Who Gets What — and Why* (2015). Accessible introduction to market design (matching, auctions, kidney exchange).
- **Krishna, V.** — *Auction Theory* (2010). Comprehensive treatment of auction formats and their properties.
- **Milgrom, P.** — *Discovering Prices: Auction Design in Markets with Complex Constraints* (2017). Modern auction design for spectrum and beyond.
- **Mas-Colell, A., Whinston, M. D., & Green, J. R.** — *Microeconomic Theory* (1995). Standard reference for the theoretical foundations.
- **Akerlof, G. A.** — "The Market for 'Lemons'" (1970), *Quarterly Journal of Economics*. How information asymmetry drives adverse selection — sellers of high-quality goods exit markets that can't distinguish quality.
- **Williamson, O. E.** — *The Economic Institutions of Capitalism* (1985). Why transactions move inside firms when asset specificity, uncertainty, and frequency make market contracting too costly.
- **Hart, O.** — *Firms, Contracts, and Financial Structure* (1995). The incomplete contracts framework: when contracts can't specify all contingencies, control rights determine outcomes.
- **Coase, R. H.** — "The Nature of the Firm" (1937) and "The Problem of Social Cost" (1960). Why firms exist (transaction costs make market coordination expensive) and how property rights assignments affect efficiency.
- **Ostrom, E.** — *Governing the Commons* (1990). Eight design principles for long-enduring commons institutions; demonstrates that neither markets nor states are necessary for sustainable resource governance.
- **Hirschman, A. O.** — *Exit, Voice, and Loyalty* (1970). How the interplay of market exit and institutional voice shapes whether organizations self-correct or degrade.

## Key Concepts

- **Incentive compatibility** — agents do best by reporting truthfully.
- **The revelation principle** — any mechanism's outcome can be replicated by a direct, truthful mechanism.
- **Matching markets** — two-sided markets (school choice, residency matching, organ exchange).
- **Metrology** — the science of measurement, critical for defining what incentive mechanisms measure and reward.
- **Adverse selection** — high-quality sellers exit when buyers can't distinguish quality (Akerlof).
- **Asset specificity** — when investments are tailored to a particular transaction, market governance becomes costly (Williamson).
- **Incomplete contracts** — contracts that cannot specify terms for all contingencies; residual control rights then determine who captures value (Hart & Moore).
- **Transaction costs** — costs of using the price mechanism: search, bargaining, enforcement (Coase).
- **Commons governance** — institutional arrangements for managing shared resources without privatization or central control (Ostrom).
