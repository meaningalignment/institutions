# Regulatory Design & Norm Governance

## Textbooks

- **Lessig, L.** — *Code: And Other Laws of Cyberspace* (2006). Four modalities of regulation: law, norms, markets, architecture.
- **Sunstein, C.** — *Nudge* (with Thaler, 2008). How institutional design shapes behavior through defaults and framing.
- **Ostrom, E.** — *Governing the Commons* (1990). How communities self-regulate shared resources without central authority.
- **Bicchieri, C.** — *The Grammar of Society: The Nature and Dynamics of Social Norms* (2006). Formal theory of how norms emerge, persist, and change.
- **Brennan, G., Eriksson, L., Goodin, R. E., & Southwood, N.** — *Explaining Norms* (2013). Philosophical analysis of the nature and function of norms.

## Tutorials & Courses

- [Regulatory Impact Analysis (OECD)](https://www.oecd.org/regreform/regulatory-policy/ria.htm) — Framework for evaluating proposed regulations.

## Key Concepts

- **Norm internalization** — when external rules become intrinsic motivation.
- **Regulatory capture** — when regulators serve the interests of the regulated.
- **Code as law** — technical architectures that enforce behavioral constraints.
- **Normative reasoning** — how agents reason about what they ought to do (vs. what they want to do).
