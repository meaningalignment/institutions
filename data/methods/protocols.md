# Protocol Design & Standards Development

## Textbooks

- **Greenstein, S.** — *How the Internet Became Commercial* (2015). How internet protocols emerged, were standardized, and shaped competition.
- **Russell, A. L.** — *Open Standards and the Digital Age* (2014). History of standards-making from telegraphs to the web.
- **Brunsson, N. & Jacobsson, B.** — *A World of Standards* (2000). How standards coordinate action across organizations and nations.
- **Simcoe, T.** — "Standard Setting Committees" (2012, in *Oxford Handbook of the Economics of Networks*). Economics of committee-based standard setting.

## Tutorials & Courses

- [IETF RFC Process](https://www.ietf.org/standards/process/) — How internet standards actually get made.
- [W3C Standards Track](https://www.w3.org/standards/) — Web standards development process.

## Key Concepts

- **Interoperability** — the ability of systems to exchange and use information without special arrangement.
- **Rough consensus and running code** — the IETF's decision-making philosophy.
- **Standards wars** — competition between incompatible standards (e.g. VHS vs Betamax, USB-C vs Lightning).
- **Network effects** — how protocol adoption creates self-reinforcing value.
